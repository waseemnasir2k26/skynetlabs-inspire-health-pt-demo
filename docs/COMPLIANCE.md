# Compliance — Inspire Health PT (Cash-Pay PT, YMYL / Medical)

Build-ready checklist. Physical therapy marketing is YMYL/medical and HIPAA-adjacent. **A FL Board of Physical Therapy + healthcare-attorney review is required before launch** — this doc reduces risk, it does not replace counsel.

> Even cash-only practices can be HIPAA-**covered entities**. Treat PHI handling and testimonials as live legal exposure.

---

## 1. Claims to avoid (no guaranteed outcomes)

- ❌ "Cure," "fix," "guaranteed results," "eliminate pain," "100% recovery"
- ✅ Use hedged, designed-intent language: **"may help," "designed to," "aims to," "many patients experience"**
- ❌ Absolute timelines as promises ("back to sport in 4 weeks")
- ✅ Frame as typical/representative ranges with a variability disclaimer
- No comparative superiority claims you can't substantiate ("best PT in Miami")

---

## 2. Testimonials — #1 HIPAA risk

> OCR fined **Complete P.T. $25,000** for posting **named patient testimonials without authorization**.

- **Do not** publish a named patient testimonial without a **signed HIPAA marketing authorization** on file.
- Preferred low-risk path: **aggregate review ratings** (e.g. "4.8★ from 120+ reviews") and/or de-identified quotes.
- Every testimonial / results section carries: **"Individual results vary."**
- No before/after photos, diagnoses, or identifying detail without written authorization.
- Keep signed authorizations on file; map each published testimonial to its authorization.

---

## 3. HIPAA-safe forms

> Standard web contact forms are **NOT HIPAA-compliant** for PHI.

- Collect **name, email, phone, and a general reason only**.
- Add a visible notice: **"Please don't submit medical details or health history through this form."**
- Route any PHI / scheduling that touches health info through a **BAA-covered scheduler** (confirm Calendly/booking vendor will sign a BAA, or restrict the embed to non-PHI intake).
- No symptom checkers, diagnosis fields, or free-text "describe your condition" boxes on the public site.

---

## 4. Out-of-network / superbill copy (mandatory framing)

Use language equivalent to:

> "Inspire Health PT is **cash-based and out-of-network**. We provide a **superbill** you can submit to your insurer for **possible reimbursement**. **Coverage is not guaranteed** and depends on your individual plan."

- Never imply we bill insurance or guarantee reimbursement.
- Pair with the qualify-then-quote pricing posture (no public guaranteed rates beyond what's intended to be public).

---

## 5. Ebook + medical disclaimers

- **Ebook (mandatory):** "This ebook is for **educational purposes only** and is **not medical advice**. Consult a licensed provider before starting any exercise or treatment program."
- **Site-wide medical disclaimer** in footer + on Services/Eval/Ebook pages: content is informational, not a substitute for professional diagnosis or treatment, no provider-patient relationship is formed by using the site.
- Ebook checkout (WooCommerce/Stripe): standard terms + refund policy for a digital product.

---

## 6. Direct access (FL "no referral")

- FL **allows direct access** to PT — market "**no referral needed**" **accurately**, noting **statutory limits** (duration/visit caps before referral may be required).
- Do not overstate scope; phrase as "you can start without a physician referral, subject to Florida's direct-access rules."

---

## 7. EEAT — mandatory

| Item | Required |
|---|---|
| DPT credentials + **FL license #** per PT | ✓ |
| Real bios: headshot, school, years, certs | ✓ |
| **NPI** per practitioner | ✓ |
| Physical address + embedded map + click-to-call | ✓ |
| Reviews (**50+ / 4.5★** target) | ✓ |
| HTTPS | ✓ |
| Privacy policy | ✓ |
| Medical disclaimer | ✓ |

**Nice-to-have trust boosters:** APTA / FPTA badges · ABPTS specialty certs (OCS / SCS) · CSCS · press mentions.
