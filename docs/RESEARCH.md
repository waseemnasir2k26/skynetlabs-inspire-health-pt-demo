# Research — Inspire Health PT (Premium Cash-Pay PT, Miami FL)

Competitor teardown, niche taste read, and the positioning thesis behind the build.

---

## 1. Niche taste read

Premium / concierge / cash-pay PT and performance clinics converge on a tight, repeatable visual + messaging playbook. The category is **homogeneous** — which is the opening.

**What every premium PT site does (table stakes):**
- Hidden or buried pricing — qualify-then-quote, no public rate card
- "Doctor, not an assistant" + "1-on-1, every session, hands-on" promise
- Press wall + star-rating trust block near the top
- Proprietary, often trademarked, method language (a named "system")
- Outcome / athlete imagery, not stock clinical photos

**The sameness gap (where they all look identical):**
- Default palette: **clinical teal / navy / cool grey**
- Default type: **generic geometric sans** (Poppins / Montserrat / Inter alone)
- Booking via **static contact form**, not a live scheduler
- Feature-led copy ("we offer dry needling") over outcome-led copy

## 3. Premium vs insurance-mill signal table

Buyers paying cash read these signals to separate a concierge clinic from a high-volume in-network mill.

| Signal | Insurance mill | Premium cash-pay (our target) |
|---|---|---|
| Who treats you | Tech / assistant, rotating | **DPT doctor, same one every visit** |
| Session format | 3–4 patients at once | **1-on-1, full hour, hands-on** |
| Booking | Phone tag / form → callback | **Live scheduler, book in 30 sec** |
| Pricing posture | "We bill your insurance" | **Cash-based, out-of-network, superbill provided** |
| Copy | Feature list of modalities | **Outcome-led ("back to your sport in N weeks")** |
| Trust | Generic 5-star widget | **Named DPT bios, credentials, press, reviews** |
| Visual | Clinical teal/navy + stock photos | **Warm editorial, athlete imagery, brand system** |

## 4. Positioning thesis — why warm-luxe wins

The category's biggest weakness is that **everyone looks the same**. When every competitor defaults to clinical teal/navy + geometric sans + a contact form, the cheapest, highest-leverage differentiator is **art direction**.

**Our wedge:**
1. **Warm serif editorial palette** (Fraunces + bronze/sage on warm paper) reads as *premium hospitality*, not *clinic* — instant separation in a teal/navy field. See `DESIGN-TOKENS.md` V1 "Editorial."
2. **Live scheduler** (Calendly embed) beats the form-based booking everyone else uses — removes friction at the exact moment of high intent.
3. **Outcome-led copy** — lead with the result the buyer wants (return to sport, pain-free, performance) over a modality feature list, within compliance limits (see `COMPLIANCE.md` — no guaranteed-outcome claims).
4. We still **match table stakes**: DPT-doctor promise, 1-on-1 framing, trust wall, qualify-then-quote pricing posture, proprietary method language.

**Recommendation:** lead the pitch with **V1 Editorial (warm-luxe)**. It is the single clearest visual departure from the competitive set and signals premium positioning before a word is read. V2 Cinematic and V3 Minimal ship as live alternates for the client to feel the range.
